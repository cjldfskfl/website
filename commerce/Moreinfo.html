Commonly Asked Questions

Training and Requirements

What do I need to do in university to become a lawyer?

There are multiple stages to pursuing an education in law. This includes a Bachelor’s of Laws(LLB), an undergraduate degree that provides the student with a foundational understanding of the career. Additionally, there's an option to conduct this study with a combined Bachelors of Laws. This is a program where a student studies law alongside another discipline. This will have a longer duration than a regular LLB. A postgraduate degree known as a Juris Doctor (JD) is also available to pepple who want to practise law in Australia and have completed an undergraduate degree in a different discipline or have a legal degree from another country.

What is the minimum level of education required for a career in law?

After completing high school you are granted eligibility to pursue a Bachelor’s degree in Laws or a combined degree. You may also complete an undergraduate degree in a different field and then pursue a postgraduate Juris Doctor and after completing your law degree, there is a mandatory Practical Legal Training (PLT) that you must complete in order to be admitted to legal practice.

How long does a conventional law degree take to complete?

A Bachelor's degree of Laws typically takes four years to complete however if you pursue a combined degree (for example a Bachelor of Laws and a Bachelor in music) this can take five to six years. Alternatively, pursuing a Juris Doctor requires three years of full-time study if you have already completed your undergraduate degree and the aforementioned Practical Legal Training.

How much does it cost to study law in Australia?

For a Bachelor of Laws or Juris Doctor degree, domestic students typically pay between AUD 10,000 and AUD 15,000 a year if they are enrolled in a Commonwealth Supported Place (CSP), where the government pays a portion of the tuition.If they are not qualified for a CSP, domestic students who pay in full may have to spend between AUD 20,000 and AUD 40,000 in fees each year.

Salary, Conditions, Perks

What is the salary of a lawyer?

Currently, the average base salary for a lawyer in Sydney, NSW is $111,634 per year, and this figure is the same as the national average for lawyers in Australia. To better understand the level of wealth associated with a legal career, we can compare it to other well-known and lucrative professions. For example, a surgeon's base salary in the same area pays an average of $121,654 annually and an occupation as a waitress pays $73,496 in Sydney NSW.

How many hours a week do lawyers work?

It's not uncommon for lawyers to work more than the standard amount of hours as the job requires work during weekends and late at night. Labour Market Insights conducted by the Australian Government reveal solicitors work an average of 48 hours per week. These statistics aren’t exactly reliable however as many small tasks such as answering client phone calls or communicating with colleagues won't count towards the stats though they are part of a lawyers job. Even with the right to disconnect being added to all awards as of August 26th, our primary sources have remarked that this hasn’t been applicable to them as their clients still expect to be able to make contact after working hours.

What are some employee perks that law firms offer?

In Australia, lawyers receive a variety of benefits intended to improve their work-life balance. Flexible work arrangements, such remote work possibilities and hybrid schedules, are common perks. In addition to performance-based awards, many firms provide opportunity for ongoing legal education for professional development. Additionally well-liked are health and wellness initiatives that provide private health insurance subsidies, mental health support, and gym memberships. In order to promote a healthy work environment, companies can also offer additional leave benefits, such as paid parental leave, and support networking events and social gatherings.

What are the financial and fiscal benefits associated with a law career?

Lawyers typically make competitive incomes; in large cities like Sydney, the average base pay rises for seasoned attorneys or those practising in niche areas (eg. intellectual property). In addition to this, many firms give top employees stock participation, profit-sharing, or bonuses. Lawyers may also receive tax-deductable travel, continued legal education and mentorship. Furthermore, companies often contribute more to superannuation than the minimal amount, improving long-term financial stability. Becoming a lawyer is a steady and financially lucrative professional choice due to the high career progression and fiscal rewards.

Conditions

Can you work from home?

As previously stated, the law occupation does offer a very flexible work style with most of the tasks associated with the career being done digitally and therefore remotely. Though it may take some time in your career before you have the ability to dictate or alter the typical workstyle, generally law firms will offer their employees flexibility in regards to where work is conducted.

What is the average span of a law career?

Depending on individual career choices and retirement age, the average length of a legal profession in Australia is normally between thirty and forty years. After finishing their education and Practical Legal Training, many lawyers start their professions in their mid-to-late 20s (PLT). Particularly for those in senior or specialised roles like partners, barristers, or judges, lawyers can continue practising long into their 60s. Continuous professional development is a major factor in the longevity of legal careers; many people in the field stay in the industry for decades due to specialisation and advancement chances.

Are there any health/lifestyle issues associated with this profession?

The legal profession poses risks to both physical and mental health, with over 50% of solicitors experiencing anxiety and 35% experiencing depression. Prolonged work hours, deadlines, and handling complex situations contribute to these issues. Sedentary lifestyles, such as poor posture, back pain, and increased heart disease risk, can also result from extended desk work. Despite the increasing focus on mental health by Australian organizations through wellness programs, managing health remains a challenge for many professionals in the legal profession.

Do lawyers get a lot of vacation?

Owing to the demanding nature of the legal profession, which frequently limits time off owing to long hours and heavy workloads, travelling on vacation can be difficult for Australian lawyers. However, depending on tenure and business policies, many law firms give yearly leave entitlements that might vary from four to six weeks. Even with hectic schedules, vacation time is essential for mental and physical health, and businesses are realising this and offering flexible leave policies and work-life balance to reflect this. Lawyers can maximise their vacation time by assigning responsibilities to others and planning vacations well in advance.

What are the career advancement opportunities?

A lawyer's career progression typically starts with junior solicitor or associate positions. After demonstrating exceptional performance, they may be promoted to senior associate status. Often, they become a specialist or senior lawyer, focusing on specialized legal fields. Partnering involves major duties like business growth and firm management, and becoming a barrister is the ultimate goal. Opportunities for career advancement include in-house counsel positions and legal consultancy. Professional development and networking are crucial for career advancement in the legal field.
